Breaking Research

Scholar Publishes Seven Papers
on Security and Machine Learning
Within a Single Academic Year

Acoustic keystrokes recovered across hardware; LLM agents jailbroken by psychology; malware classified in curved space — an astonishing year of inquiry at IIIT-Delhi.

By B. B. P. Maurya  ·  IIIT-Delhi Correspondent

In an era when the boundaries between security and machine learning grow ever more indistinct, one young researcher at the Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology has produced a body of work that demands the attention of the field.

Mr. Bikrant Bikram Pratap Maurya, a second-year M.Tech candidate, has this year seen four papers accepted at international venues of consequence — AsiaCCS, ICME, ICASSP, and AACL-IJCNLP — while submitting three further manuscripts to Interspeech and the ACM Conference on Computer and Communications Security.

"Security is the lens. Machine learning is the instrument. Every system must be designed as though the adversary has read the same papers."

His research spans four principal territories: the reconstruction of keystrokes from acoustic emanations propagated across heterogeneous hardware; the susceptibility of large-language-model agents to psychology-informed jailbreak strategies; the classification of malware via hyperbolic cross-attention in curved geometric space; and the detection of conversational deception through positive-unlabelled learning.

A unifying thread runs through each body of work. Rather than treating threat models as an afterthought, Mr. Maurya insists on designing the adversary before designing the model — an approach that has yielded results robust to conditions that defeat contemporary baselines.

His DECKER framework, accepted at AsiaCCS 2026, achieves zero-shot transfer across unseen keyboard hardware — a milestone that prior domain-adaptation methods could not reach. His hyperbolic fusion work, appearing at ICASSP and ICME, demonstrates that the geometry of representation space is not a mere convenience but a principled choice with measurable consequences.

Side-Channel Intelligence
Acoustic Side-Channel Attacks
The reconstruction of keystrokes from the sounds a keyboard produces — across Wi-Fi, over VoIP, on hardware never seen during training. Domain-invariant representations, zero-shot transfer, continuous sparse transduction over distorted channels.
Agent Integrity
LLM Agent Security & Jailbreaks
Psychology-informed adversarial prompts — authority, urgency, social pressure — deployed against tool-augmented multi-agent systems. Schema-bound defences, Trust-Decay effects, semantic steganography in enterprise pipelines.
Adversarial Learning
Adversarial Machine Learning
Threat-aware model design from first principles. Domain shift treated not as nuisance but as active adversary. Representation learning that holds under attack — not merely on clean benchmarks where no adversary is present.
Non-Euclidean Geometry
Hyperbolic Representation Learning
Curved-space geometry for hierarchical and compositional structure — where Euclidean embeddings break down and the Poincaré ball takes over. Applied to malware classification, LLM code attribution, and multimodal information fusion.
